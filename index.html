My research uses topology and symmetry to understand and engineer complex optical and soft-matter systems. I study topological defects in liquid crystals through group-theoretic and quantum field theoretic viewpoints, building effective descriptions of textures, defect-mediated transitions, and field-driven dynamics. Alongside this, I work on photonic integrated circuits for scalable optical functionality, from sub-Rayleigh imaging via coherence and speckle control to programmable architectures for optical neural networks.
