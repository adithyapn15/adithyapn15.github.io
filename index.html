My research uses topology and symmetry to understand and engineer complex optical and soft-matter systems. I study topological defects in liquid crystals through group-theoretic and quantum field theoretic viewpoints, building effective descriptions of textures, defect-mediated transitions, and field-driven dynamics. Alongside this, I work on photonic integrated circuits for scalable optical functionality, from sub-Rayleigh imaging via coherence and speckle control to programmable architectures for optical neural networks. The aim is to connect mathematical structure to device-level performance through modelling, fabrication, and measurement.

Currently: University of Oxford (Soft Matter Photonics Group); projects on topology, defects, photonic integrated circuits, holographic microscopy, smart windows, and blue-phase devices.
Previously: Columbia University (Applied Physics and Applied Mathematics); projects on thin-film transistors, laser-annealed phase transitions, and racetrack memory concepts. Earlier work includes structural materials, metallic glasses, and corrosion.
